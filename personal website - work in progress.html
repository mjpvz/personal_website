Researcher, Psychologist, Developer and all-round Nerd.
I’m a cognitive psychologist, currently working in the field of human material perception at the Technical University of Delft. I’ve always been facinated by the human brain and the way we experience and perceive the world around us. I enjoy using technology and aim for innovative solutions in my work. Self-improvement is - and always has been - important to me; I’m motivated to be the best I can be. As a result, I enjoy learning new things and I love the thrill of discovery. Outside of work, I'm always listing to music and I love traveling and seeing new places. I'm an avid reader and devour practically everything, from the oldest classics to the newest science fiction. If there’s one word to describe me, it’s curious.

My skills

Researcher I have a background in pyschology, where I was always interestesed in perception. I am currently working in the field of human visual perception, with a focus on material perception. My natural curiosity, combined with my analytical mindset keeps me commited and motivated to make my mark in this field. I like encorperating technology into my research, as illustrated by my self-taught experience with programming and coding.

Developer I am a self-taught coder and have written code used in a variety of projects such as VR experiments, fMRI- EEG- and EMG-studies. My current research project used a big-data approach for which I collected data from thousands of citizen scientists over the internet. I have a preference for using Python, but depending on the project I’m comfortable with using Matlab and JavaScript (and html,css). If need be, I can make some minor changes to some C source-code .

Languages I’m a native Dutch speaker, I’m fluent in English and I usually manage to order the right food in German, French and Spanish.
